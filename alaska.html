Conclusion

While social media speculation about a Putin body double at the Alaska summit gained traction, the weight of evidence suggests the Russian leader was indeed present. The physical differences noted by observers can largely be attributed to aging, camera angles, and normal human variation. The high-stakes nature of the meeting, advanced security screening, and lack of concrete evidence make the body double theory unlikely despite its viral spread online.

Kremlin spokesperson Dmitry Peskov dismissed the claims as "yet another lie," noting that those who work with Putin "can hardly keep up with him." While the theory persists in certain circles, most experts consider it a conspiracy theory without substantive evidence.
